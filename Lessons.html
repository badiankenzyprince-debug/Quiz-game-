Lesson 1: Vocabulary

Vocabulary helps you understand and express ideas clearly. Try to use these words in sentences.

Improve To make something better
Confident Feeling sure about yourself
Practice Doing something again and again to get better
Skill An ability you learn and develop
Mistake Something done wrong

Example Sentences:

  • I want to improve my English.
  • She feels confident when she speaks.
  • Practice helps you learn faster.

Lesson 2: Grammar – Present Simple

The Present Simple Tense is used to talk about:

  • Daily routines
  • Habits
  • Facts and general truths

Sentence Structure

Subject + Verb + Object

  • I study English.
  • You read books.
  • They practice speaking.

For He / She / It

Add -s or -es to the verb.

  • He studies English.
  • She watches TV.
  • It works well.

Negative Sentences

  • I do not like coffee.
  • She does not understand the lesson.

Lesson 3: Reading Practice

Learning English is a journey. You do not need to be perfect. Every day, you can learn something new by reading, listening, and speaking.

Many students feel afraid of making mistakes, but mistakes are a natural part of learning. When you make mistakes, you learn what to improve. Confidence grows when you practice regularly.

Try to read short texts every day. Read slowly, understand the meaning, and say the words out loud if possible. This will help your pronunciation and speaking skills.

Lesson 4: Daily English Practice Tips

  • Read English for 10–15 minutes every day
  • Listen to English songs or videos
  • Speak English in front of a mirror
  • Write short sentences about your day
  • Do not be afraid of mistakes
